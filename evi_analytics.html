IBM Analytic Server

The IBM Cognos Analytic Server (ICAS) stores data that can be accessed by client software users. This allows users to model and manage data independently of the IBM Cognos Express server products that are installed. It also manages interactive requests to run reports and provides output.



Video Analytics

Intelligent Video Analytics helps develop a comprehensive threat model customized to the environment. It can identify perimeter breaches, as well as abandoned objects, objects removed, and people and vehicle activity. The Intelligent Video Analytics framework can also integrate specialized analytics such as license plate and facial recognition. Thus IBM IVA gives court room participants and suspects’ analysis capability to Evidencer.

IBM Intelligent Video analytics assists in performing motion analysis, while IBM I2 Coplink provides an edge with the facial recognition feature.



Intelligence Report

Crime Department and Judiciary can now benefit from analysis about crimes reported and various cases with report generation support in Evidencer. Analyse and change your strategy based on how effective case sessions have been.

Law and Enforcement along with Judiciary can provide Justice at the right time based on keen evidence and reports collected and analysed using Evidencer. IBM Cognos BI gives this report generation capability to Evidencer.

Analytic reports on viewing statistics which includes who viewed, what all files were viewed, number of times it is viewed and how long, etc. Evidencer Analytics can be integrated with Google Analytics. Reports can be exported, analysed and are downloadable.

EviAnalytics utilizes IBM’s Cognos BI technology for performance management, strategy management and analytics applications. Various reports generated include:

  • Statistical Analytic Report: User can enter the case type like civil, criminal, family court case, probate case etc. Based on the case type enter a graph gets displayed based on number of cases vs. year.
  • Satisfactory Analytic Report: Report based on the rating given to the judge for his judgement.
  • Most Popular case: List with most popular case for that year.
  • Cases with maximum hearings: Case number vs. number of hearings for that case.
